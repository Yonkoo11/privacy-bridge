How It Works

01
Lock funds

Deposit a fixed denomination into the Privacy Bridge contract. A Poseidon commitment is inserted into the on-chain Merkle tree. No link between your address and the commitment is exposed.

Flow EVM
02
Generate proof

Off-chain, produce a Groth16 proof that you know a secret and nullifier corresponding to a leaf in the Merkle tree, without revealing which leaf. The circuit has 6,634 constraints.

Client-side
03
Relay root

The current Merkle root is relayed from Flow to Starknet. A ring buffer of 30 roots ensures users have time to submit proofs against recent state.

Cross-chain
04
Claim on Starknet

Submit the proof to the Starknet bridge contract. The garaga verifier checks the BN254 pairing on-chain. Funds are minted to the recipient. The nullifier is marked spent to prevent double claims.

Starknet

Privacy Properties

  • [PASS] Fixed denomination pools — Deposits are restricted to 0.0001, 0.001, 0.01, or 0.1 FLOW. Equal amounts prevent value-based correlation between deposits and withdrawals.
  • [PASS] On-chain Merkle tree — Poseidon hash, depth 24, supporting ~16 million leaves. Root ring buffer of 30 entries prevents front-running invalidation.
  • [PASS] Commitment hiding — commitment = Poseidon(Poseidon(secret, nullifier), amount). The preimage is never published. Only the prover knows the link.
  • [PASS] No privacy-leaking events — Transfer events emit only the amount. No from/to addresses. No CID references. Observers see deposits but cannot link them to withdrawals.
  • [PASS] Relayer support with fee protection — Users can submit through a relayer to avoid revealing their Starknet address. max_fee_bps parameter prevents relayers from extracting more than the user agreed to.
  • [PASS] Withdrawal timelock — Roots carry timestamps. Withdrawals must wait a minimum delay, increasing the anonymity set before funds can be claimed.
  • [PASS] Emergency withdraw — 30-day timelock on emergency withdrawal. Owner cannot rug instantly. Users have time to exit via normal proof path.
